// What is Gamification in the Classroom?
Definition

Gamification

The application of game elements and mechanics — points, levels, challenges, narrative — to non-game contexts like a classroom. The goal is to maximize student engagement, motivation, and intrinsic interest in the material.

Why it works

The Psychology

Games tap into intrinsic motivation through clear goals, immediate feedback, and a sense of progress. When course content is framed as a challenge to overcome rather than material to memorize, retention and engagement improve significantly.

Example 1

Profit or Peril

An accounting course gamified as a D&D-inspired roleplay — students make financial decisions as characters, with consequences that mirror real accounting outcomes. Risk-taking and strategy become part of the learning loop.

Example 2

CLI Dungeon Crawl

A text-based dungeon game built on a simulated command line interface — used in a Digital Literacy course to teach CLI navigation. Students explore rooms by typing real commands, learning the terminal without realizing they are studying.
