⚠ Where does SCM fit in the TLC? Supply Chain Management (SCM) does not sit inside the TLC as a stage. SCM is a broader management framework that wraps around the entire chain — it includes the supplier at the start and the customer at the end, both of which are outside the TLC boundary. The TLC describes the internal logistics flow only. SCM coordinates everything across the full picture.
// Concept Conflict — JIT vs Globalization
Just-In-Time (JIT)
  • Minimize inventory held
  • Reduce lead times
  • Order only what you need, when you need it
  • Requires reliable, nearby suppliers
  • Goal: zero waste, zero excess
Globalization
  • Suppliers spread across the globe
  • Increases lead times significantly
  • Requires larger safety stock buffers
  • More variables: ports, customs, delays
  • Goal: cost efficiency through scale
The conflict: JIT tries to minimize inventory and lead time. Globalization naturally increases both. They have fundamentally different goals — JIT demands speed and proximity, while globalization trades both for cost savings. This is why global supply chains are often described as "fragile" — COVID-19 exposed exactly this tension.
